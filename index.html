The case, retold

Every market has a snitch.

The old problem: the moment your order hits the mempool, a bot reads it and trades ahead of you. The idea here: don't let anyone read it. Seal every order, open them all at once, and the advantage disappears with the visibility.

What happens on a broken seal

01

A 2.9% fee applies to any attempt to cancel or resubmit an order to dodge the queue — treated as tampering with the file.

02

Tampering fees route to the reveal rewards pool, paid out to wallets that held their sealed order to term.

03

Batches with zero tampering carry a small bonus, funded from the protocol treasury, split across that batch's participants.
